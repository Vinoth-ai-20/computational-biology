# Module 20 — Papers

Five foundational papers that underpin the capstone narrative. Read all of these
before Month 10 so the capstone writeups can cite them meaningfully.

| Paper | Type | Why it matters | When to read | Difficulty | Prerequisites |
|-------|------|----------------|-------------|------------|---------------|
| Love et al. 2014 (DESeq2) | Methods | The standard for RNA-seq DE analysis; CP01's reference implementation | Month 4 (Module 10) | Medium | Module 03 (statistics), Module 09 |
| Alipanahi et al. 2015 (DeepBind) | Methods | The CNN-for-TF-binding paper that CP02 reimplements | Month 8 (Module 14) | Medium | Module 13, Module 14 |
| Kermack & McKendrick 1927 (SIR) | Foundational | The original compartmental epidemic model; CP03's mathematical basis | Month 6 (Module 15) | Low | Module 02 (ODEs) |
| Wilson et al. 2017 (Good Enough Practices) | Perspective | Direct guide to how to structure the portfolio repository | Month 1 | Low | None |
| Jumper et al. 2021 (AlphaFold2) | Landmark | The highest-impact recent paper in computational biology; critical for PhD application narrative | Month 10 (Module 14) | High | Module 14 NB11 |
