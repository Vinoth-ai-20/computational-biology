# Papers — Module 02: Mathematics for Biology

## Reading Schedule

| Paper | Type | Why it matters | When to read | Difficulty | Prerequisites |
|-------|------|---------------|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| Strogatz, *Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos* §1–§3 | Classic book (chapters) | Core reference for ODEs, fixed points, phase portraits — read these three chapters, not the full book | Week 3–4 | Moderate | NB02–03 done |
| May (1976), *Simple mathematical models with very complicated dynamics*, Nature | Foundational | Introduced the logistic map and chaos — directly connected to NB05; short and readable | Week 3 | Easy | NB04–05 done |
| Murray (2002), *Mathematical Biology I* Chapter 1 | Classic book (chapter) | Canonical introduction to ODE modelling in biology; read §1.1–§1.3 only | Week 4 | Moderate | NB06 done |

## Optional / Extension

| Paper | Type | Why | When |
|-------|------|-----|------|
| Edelstein-Keshet, *Mathematical Models in Biology* §1 | Classic book | Alternative framing; more detailed than Murray on discrete models | After NB05 |
| Volterra (1928), *Variations and Fluctuations of the Number of Individuals in Animal Species* | Foundational (historical) | Original Lotka-Volterra paper — read for historical context, not mathematical detail | After NB09 |
