# Module 03 Papers — Statistics and Probability

## Reading List

| Paper | Type | Why it matters | When to read | Difficulty | Prerequisites |
|-------|------|---------------|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| Benjamini & Hochberg (1995), *Controlling the False Discovery Rate: a practical and powerful approach to multiple testing*, J. R. Stat. Soc. B | **Foundational** | The FDR correction every genomics analysis depends on; cited in every DESeq2/edgeR paper | After NB11 | Moderate | Hypothesis testing (NB05) |
| Ioannidis (2005), *Why Most Published Research Findings Are False*, PLOS Medicine | **Classic** | Short (6 pages), sobering, directly relevant to p-value interpretation; builds statistical humility before forming habits | After NB10 | Easy | NB05 |
| Efron & Tibshirani, *An Introduction to the Bootstrap*, Ch. 1–5 | **Reference** | The canonical treatment of bootstrap methods; Ch. 1–5 are sufficient | After NB14 | Moderate | Probability (NB03) |
| Storey & Tibshirani (2003), *Statistical significance for genomewide studies*, PNAS | **Foundational** | Introduces q-value / FDR at the whole-genome scale; bridges NB11 to real genomics practice | After NB11 | Moderate | BH correction (NB11) |
