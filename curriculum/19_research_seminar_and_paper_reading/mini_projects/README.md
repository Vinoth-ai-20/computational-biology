# Module 19 — Mini Projects

This module is Ongoing/Cross-cutting and does not have traditional mini projects. However, the following extended practice exercises serve the same function of producing portfolio-quality artefacts.

## Mini Project 19-1: Paper-Notes Repository

**What:** A populated `paper-notes/` directory at the repository root, with at least 12 paper notes in the Three-Pass format (one per month of the programme).
**Portfolio value:** Directly visible on GitHub. European PhD supervisors often look at a candidate's paper-reading evidence. A dated, structured log of 12+ papers with Pass-3 binary outcomes is concrete evidence of independent scientific engagement.
**Timeline:** Begins Week 1, maintained throughout.

## Mini Project 19-2: Benchmark Validity Scorecard

**What:** Apply the NB04 benchmark scoring rubric to 3 real papers from your current module. Write up the scores and flag any validity concerns. Submit to `mini_projects/benchmark_audit.md`.
**Portfolio value:** Demonstrates critical evaluation of computational methods — valued by PhD supervisors who care about rigour.
**Timeline:** Complete by Month 4 (after Module 13 machine learning context exists).

## Mini Project 19-3: Research Map

**What:** A visual research map of your target sub-field, built using the LiteratureMap class from NB07 and exported as a static figure. Submit to `mini_projects/research_map.png` with a one-page explanatory `research_map_notes.md`.
**Portfolio value:** Visual evidence of field understanding; directly usable in PhD application letters ("I have mapped the landscape of X and see the following gap...").
**Timeline:** Complete by Month 9 (after the Month 6 specialization checkpoint has narrowed the target).
