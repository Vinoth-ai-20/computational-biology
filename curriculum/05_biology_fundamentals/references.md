# Module 05 — References

## Books

| Title | Authors | Role | Chapters to prioritise |
|-------|---------|------|------------------------|
| *Molecular Biology of the Cell* (7th ed.) | Alberts et al. | The canonical reference — pull specific chapters, never read cover-to-cover | Ch 1 (cells), Ch 4 (DNA), Ch 6 (gene expression), Ch 7 (gene control) |
| *Genetics: A Conceptual Approach* (7th ed.) | Pierce | More accessible entry point than Alberts; better for a first pass | Ch 2–5 (Mendelian), Ch 16–18 (population genetics) |
| *Principles of Population Genetics* (4th ed.) | Hartl & Clark | The Hardy-Weinberg and drift reference | Ch 1–3 (HWE, drift, selection) |
| *Molecular Evolution* | Li | Population genetics at molecular level | Ch 2 (neutral theory) — after Module 06 |
| *An Introduction to Bioinformatics Algorithms* | Jones & Pevzner | Bridge from biology to computation | Ch 2–3 — reference for Module 08 |

## Video / Interactive Resources

| Resource | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Khan Academy Biology | Best free resource for zero-background entry; covers NB02–NB08 |
| Amoeba Sisters (YouTube) | Short, visual, excellent for vocabulary and mental models |

## Key Software Used in This Module

| Tool | Role |
|------|------|
| `numpy` | Wright-Fisher simulation, HWE calculations |
| `matplotlib` / `seaborn` | Cell diagrams, allele frequency plots, phylogenetic trees |
| `scipy.stats` | Chi-square test for HWE deviation |
| `biopython` | Sequence translation, codon tables (NB03) |
| `networkx` | Food web graphs (NB11), Boolean gene regulation (NB10) |
