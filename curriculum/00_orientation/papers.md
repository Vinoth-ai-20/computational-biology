# Reading List

This module focuses on developing scientific literacy rather than Computational Biology knowledge.

---

## Paper Reading Objectives

Learn how to:

* Read scientific papers efficiently.
* Extract the main contribution.
* Identify methodology.
* Understand figures.
* Critically evaluate results.
* Write literature notes.

---

## Recommended Reading

### 1. How to Read a Paper

**Author:** S. Keshav

Type: Methodology

Difficulty: Beginner

Why read?

A classic guide introducing the Three-Pass Method for reading research papers efficiently.

---

### 2. The Craft of Research

**Authors:** Wayne C. Booth, Gregory G. Colomb, Joseph M. Williams

Type: Research methodology

Difficulty: Beginner

---

### 3. Good Enough Practices in Scientific Computing

**Authors:** Wilson et al.

Type: Scientific Computing

Difficulty: Beginner

Why read?

Introduces reproducible research, version control, documentation, and collaborative scientific programming.

---

## Literature Notes

For every paper, record:

* Citation
* Research question
* Key contributions
* Methods
* Results
* Limitations
* Personal reflections
* Ideas for future exploration

---

## Completion Criteria

Before starting Module 01, you should have:

* Read at least one scientific paper using the Three-Pass Method.
* Written one complete literature note.
* Understood how scientific papers are organized.
* Established a reproducible workflow for reading and documenting research.
