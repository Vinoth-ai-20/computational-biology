# Module 07 — Biochemistry and Molecular Biology

**Depth tier:** Tier 2 — Working competence  
**Week budget:** Weeks 9–14 (parallel with Modules 06 and 08)  
**Notebooks:** 10  
**Status:** ⏳ Not started

---

## Motivation

Biochemistry is the molecular foundation of everything downstream. Understanding
enzyme kinetics explains why drug IC50 curves look the way they do. Understanding
the mechanics of transcription and translation explains why RNA-seq reads pile up
at splice junctions. Understanding PTMs explains why Western blots and mass spectra
look the way they do. This module builds the vocabulary and quantitative intuition
to read wet-lab adjacent papers critically — not to become a biochemist, but to
never be lost when one is presenting.

---

## Depth tier — what this means in practice

Tier 2 (Working competence) means:
- Can use standard concepts correctly and explain what they do
- Can implement Michaelis-Menten kinetics from scratch and fit data
- Can read a biochemistry methods section and understand what assay was run and why
- **Not** required: deriving enzyme kinetics from first principles, detailed
  structural biochemistry, or wet-lab protocol knowledge

---

## Track A relevance

Wet-lab adjacent RA postings (common in CSIR/ICMR institutes and private biotech) expect
basic biochemistry literacy — enzyme kinetics, Western blot interpretation, understanding
of protein expression systems. This module ensures that gap is covered. Direct interview
signal: "explain what Km means" is a documented biochemistry RA interview question.

## Track B relevance

Structural biology (Module 11) and proteomics (Module 10) both assume this material
as background. Reading AlphaFold papers without understanding protein primary structure
or PTMs is possible but slow — this module removes that friction.
