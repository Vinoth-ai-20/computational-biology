# Module 07 — Papers

Tier 2 module — papers are for context and landmark awareness, not deep Pass-3 reconstruction.
Two key papers are noted for Pass-3 practice; the rest are Pass-1/Pass-2.

---

| Paper | Type | Why it matters | When to read | Difficulty | Prereqs |
|-------|------|---------------|-------------|-----------|---------|
| Michaelis & Menten (1913), *Die Kinetik der Invertinwirkung* | Foundational (historic) | The original derivation of what became the MM equation (in German; read the English translation by Goody & Johnson 2011, Biochemistry) | Week 10, with NB03 | Easy once you know the result | None |
| Briggs & Haldane (1925), *A note on the kinetics of enzyme action*, Biochemical J. | Foundational | The steady-state derivation that replaced Michaelis's equilibrium assumption — this is what "MM kinetics" actually is today | Week 10, with NB03 | Easy | Michaelis & Menten |
| Alberts et al., Chapter 6 excerpt — Transcription | Reference | The clearest summary of RNA Pol II mechanism | Week 11, with NB05 | Moderate | Module 05 |
| Protein Data Bank: Berman et al. (2000), *The Protein Data Bank*, Nucleic Acids Res. | Reference | The PDB paper — cite when using any PDB data | Week 9 | Easy | None |
| Chou & Fasman (1978), *Prediction of protein conformation*, Biochemistry | Classic | Historic secondary structure prediction — motivates why sequence predicts structure | Week 10, with NB02 | Moderate | NB02 |

## Pass-3 reconstruction targets for this module

1. **Michaelis-Menten derivation:** given the rapid equilibrium assumption, derive
   v = Vmax·[S]/(Km+[S]) from first principles. This is the single most common
   biochemistry derivation asked in interviews. Do this with the notebook closed.

2. **Lineweaver-Burk:** explain how double-reciprocal transformation converts the
   MM hyperbola into a line, and what the x-intercept, y-intercept, and slope
   each represent.
