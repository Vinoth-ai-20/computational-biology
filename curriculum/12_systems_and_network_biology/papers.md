# Module 12 — Key Papers

Tier 2 target: ~5 papers. Pass 1 all; Pass 2–3 on the two starred papers.

| Paper | Type | Why it matters | When to read | Difficulty | Prerequisites |
|-------|------|---------------|--------------|------------|---------------|
| ★ Barabási & Albert 1999 — "Emergence of scaling in random networks" *Science* 286:509–512. DOI: 10.1126/science.286.5439.509 | Foundational | The paper that introduced the preferential attachment model and scale-free networks. 3 pages. Central to NB04 and every biological network discussion. | After NB03–NB04 | Easy (minimal math; conceptual) | NB03 |
| ★ Alon 2007 — "Network motifs: theory and experimental approaches" *Nature Rev. Genetics* 8:450–461. DOI: 10.1038/nrg2102 | Review | Best introduction to network motifs (feedforward loops, autoregulation) and why they recur in biological networks. Directly supports NB08. | After NB07–NB08 | Easy-Medium | NB02, NB08 |
| Watts & Strogatz 1998 — "Collective dynamics of 'small-world' networks" *Nature* 393:440–442. DOI: 10.1038/30918 | Foundational | The original small-world network paper. 3 pages. Clustering + short path = small world. | After NB03 | Easy | NB03 |
| Barabási et al. 2011 — "Network medicine: a network-based approach to human disease" *Nature Rev. Genetics* 12:56–68. DOI: 10.1038/nrg2918 | Review | Lays out the disease module hypothesis and network-based drug target identification. Directly supports NB11. | After NB07, NB11 | Medium | NB05–NB07 |
| Orth, Thiele & Palsson 2010 — "What is flux balance analysis?" *Nature Biotechnology* 28:245–248. DOI: 10.1038/nbt.1614 | Review | 3-page conceptual explanation of FBA — the best introduction. | Before NB10 | Easy | NB10 setup |
