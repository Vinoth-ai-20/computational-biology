# Module 11 — Structural Biology

**Depth tier:** 3 — Survey  
**Week budget:** Weeks 27–30 (4 weeks)  
**Notebooks:** 8  
**Mini-project:** None (Tier 3)

## Objectives

Survey the field well enough to read AlphaFold papers, participate in structural
biology conversations in an interview or seminar, and understand structure-based
drug design at a conceptual level. Implement lightweight PDB parsing and structure
analysis from scratch to avoid treating this as pure memorization.

## Why this helps Track A

Interviewers for bioinformatics roles increasingly ask about AlphaFold and protein
structure prediction — even for non-structural-biology positions. Being able to explain
how PDB files are organized, what cryo-EM is, and what AlphaFold2 changed distinguishes
candidates who read broadly. Basic PDB parsing is a common coding screen question.

## Why this helps Track B

EMBL, DKFZ, and several IMPRS schools have structural biology or computational
structural biology openings. Even for non-structural positions, reviewers expect
a working knowledge of AlphaFold2 and its implications. Module 11 provides
the vocabulary for this without requiring Tier 1 depth.
