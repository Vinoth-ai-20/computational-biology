# Module 11 — References

## Core Textbooks

- **Branden & Tooze — Introduction to Protein Structure (2nd ed., 1999)**  
  Classic accessible introduction; still the best narrative for beginners.

- **Lesk — Introduction to Bioinformatics (4th ed., 2014)**  
  Chapter on structure databases and comparison.

- **Fersht — Structure and Mechanism in Protein Science (1999)**  
  For the thermodynamics of protein folding if going deeper.

## Key Software and Databases

- **Protein Data Bank (PDB):** rcsb.org — primary structure repository  
  mmCIF and legacy PDB format; >200,000 deposited structures
- **Biopython (Bio.PDB):** Python library for PDB parsing and structure manipulation  
  `pip install biopython`
- **PyMOL:** Structure visualization (free open-source version: pymol.org)
- **AlphaFold Database:** alphafold.ebi.ac.uk — predicted structures for UniProt proteins
- **DSSP:** Dictionary of Secondary Structure of Proteins — standard SS assignment
- **SCOP2:** Structural Classification of Proteins — curated hierarchy of folds
- **CATH:** Class, Architecture, Topology, Homologous superfamily — automated classification

## Key Web Resources

- RCSB PDB training portal: pdb101.rcsb.org (excellent free educational resources)
- EBI EMBL Structural Biology resources: ebi.ac.uk/pdbe
- CASP results: predictioncenter.org (benchmark for structure prediction)

## Survey Articles Worth Reading at Tier 3

- Jumper et al. 2021 (AlphaFold2) — *Nature* — required reading  
- Callaway 2020 — "What's next for the protein-folding revolution" — *Nature* News  
- Berman et al. 2000 — The Protein Data Bank — *Nucleic Acids Research*
