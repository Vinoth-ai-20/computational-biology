# Module 11 — Key Papers

Tier 3 target: 2–3 papers. Read Pass 1 all three; Pass 2–3 the AlphaFold2 paper.

| Paper | Type | Why it matters | When to read | Difficulty | Prerequisites |
|-------|------|---------------|--------------|------------|---------------|
| Jumper et al. 2021 — "Highly accurate protein structure prediction with AlphaFold" *Nature* 596:583–589. DOI: 10.1038/s41586-021-03819-2 | Foundational (paradigm shift) | Solved the 50-year protein folding problem. Every bioinformatics interview in 2024–2027 will reference this. Must be able to explain Evoformer, pLDDT, and what changed. | After NB06 | Medium (architecture survey is accessible; SE(3)-equivariant module is hard) | NB03–NB05 for PDB/contact map context |
| Berman et al. 2000 — "The Protein Data Bank" *Nucleic Acids Research* 28(1):235–242. DOI: 10.1093/nar/28.1.235 | Classic/Reference | The primary citation for PDB. Explains the database design, file formats, and curation policy. Short and readable. | After NB03 | Easy | NB03 |
| Ramachandran, Ramakrishnan & Sasisekharan 1963 — "Stereochemistry of polypeptide chain configurations" *J. Mol. Biol.* 7(1):95–99. DOI: 10.1016/S0022-2836(63)80023-6 | Classic/Foundational | The original Ramachandran plot paper. 4 pages. Demonstrates how steric clash constraints define allowed backbone conformations — a pure geometric argument. | After NB04 | Easy | NB04 dihedral angle section |
