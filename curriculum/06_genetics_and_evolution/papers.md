# Module 06 — Papers

**Reading standard for Tier 2:** Pass 1 and Pass 2 for all papers; Pass 3 for at least
one (your choice — pick the one closest to your future specialisation direction).

| Paper | Type | Why it matters | When to read | Difficulty | Prerequisites |
|-------|------|---------------|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| Kimura (1968). "Evolutionary rate at the molecular level." *Nature* 217:624–626. DOI: 10.1038/217624a0 | Classic | The neutral theory — explains why most molecular variants are neutral; foundational for population genomics | After NB04 | ★★☆ | Module 05 NB07–NB08 |
| Felsenstein (1981). "Evolutionary trees from DNA sequences: a maximum likelihood approach." *J. Molecular Evolution* 17:368–376. | Foundational | The statistical framework behind modern ML phylogenetics; worth a slow read even at Tier 2 | After NB07 | ★★★ | Module 03 statistics basics |
| Kingman (1982). "The coalescent." *Stochastic Processes and Their Applications* 13:235–248. | Foundational theory | The mathematical basis of coalescent theory — at this tier, read for intuition, not formal proof | After NB05 | ★★★ | None required, but hard |
| Maynard Smith & Price (1973). "The logic of animal conflict." *Nature* 246:15–18. DOI: 10.1038/246015a0 | Classic | Introduced the ESS concept and evolutionary game theory | After NB09 | ★☆☆ | None |
| Nielsen & Wakeley (2001). "Distinguishing migration from isolation." *Genetics* 158:885–896. | Modern | Shows how to use coalescent models to infer population history — a template for reading this class of paper | After NB05 | ★★☆ | Coalescent intuition (NB05) |
| Pagel (1999). "Inferring the historical patterns of biological evolution." *Nature* 401:877–884. DOI: 10.1038/44766 | Review | Clear review of comparative methods and ancestral state reconstruction | After NB10 | ★★☆ | NB06, NB07 |

---

**Recommended first paper (Pass 1 + Pass 2):** Kimura (1968) — 2 pages, foundational,
directly builds on NB07 from Module 05, and routinely cited in population genetics papers.

**Recommended Pass 3 paper:** Maynard Smith & Price (1973) — short enough to reconstruct
fully; the ESS derivation is elementary game theory; good interview topic.
