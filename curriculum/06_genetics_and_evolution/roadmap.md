# Module 06 — Genetics and Evolution: Roadmap

**Tier:** 2 — Working Competence | **Total notebooks:** 12 | **Weeks:** 8–14

---

## Part 1 — Inheritance Beyond the Basics (Weeks 8–9)

- [ ] **NB01** — `01_why_genetics_evolution_beyond_module05.ipynb`
  Framing: what Module 05 covered vs. what this module adds; the linkage between
  classical genetics and molecular genomics; orientation to the research literature.

- [ ] **NB02** — `02_mendelian_edge_cases_linkage_epistasis.ipynb`
  Linkage and recombination; genetic maps; epistasis; incomplete penetrance; locus
  heterogeneity. Why GWAS needs to correct for LD.

- [ ] **NB03** — `03_molecular_basis_of_mutation.ipynb`
  Mutation types (point, indel, structural); mutagenesis mechanisms; DNA repair
  pathways; somatic vs. germline mutations; mutation rate estimation.

---

## Part 2 — Population Genetics: Deeper Models (Weeks 9–11)

- [ ] **NB04** — `04_genetic_drift_selection_revisited.ipynb`
  Extended Wright-Fisher: multiple alleles, selection + drift interaction, fixation
  probability of selected alleles, sweep signatures.

- [ ] **NB05** — `05_coalescent_theory_intuition.ipynb`
  The coalescent as the time-reversal of the WF model; Kingman's coalescent; MRCA;
  how effective population size shapes the coalescent; applications to genealogy and
  ancient DNA.

---

## Part 3 — Phylogenetics (Weeks 10–12)

- [ ] **NB06** — `06_phylogenetic_trees_distance_methods.ipynb`
  Distance matrices from alignments; UPGMA (review from Module 05, now with
  bootstrap); Neighbour-Joining algorithm from scratch; rooting strategies.

- [ ] **NB07** — `07_phylogenetic_trees_character_methods.ipynb`
  Maximum parsimony; the Felsenstein zone; introduction to maximum likelihood
  (conceptual only — no implementation); model selection (AIC/BIC for substitution models).

- [ ] **NB08** — `08_molecular_clocks.ipynb`
  Strict vs. relaxed molecular clocks; calibration using fossils or known events;
  rate variation across lineages; limitations and common misinterpretations.

---

## Part 4 — Evolutionary Theory (Weeks 12–13)

- [ ] **NB09** — `09_evolutionary_game_theory.ipynb`
  Prisoner's dilemma; Hawk-Dove game; Evolutionarily Stable Strategy (ESS);
  replicator dynamics; links to cooperation in biology and ALife literature.

- [ ] **NB10** — `10_convergent_and_divergent_evolution.ipynb`
  Case studies: echolocation in bats/dolphins, eye evolution; detecting convergence
  computationally (dN/dS, ancestral sequence reconstruction concepts).
